The Hurdy Gurdy and Flute

The hurdy gurdy is a string instrument that has existed since the Byzantine Empire. It sounds like a bagpipe, but has no strings, and creates sound via a rotating wheel. The flute is a woodwind instrument, except it does not contain reeds, like the saxophone or clarinet. Therefore, it is considered an aerophone, or reedless wind instrument It has existed for up 43,000 to 35,000 years ago, and has been found in the Swabian Jurian region of present day Germany. The flute uses keys and breath from the player to create different notes, which range from A to G. There are many variations of flutes, including side-blown and end-blown varieties.
